About Me

Education and Interests

My interest in computer science first started in junior high, when I enrolled for a game development class. We started with a brief introduction to C++, but soon switched to using a game engine. I was fascinated by the power of code and the creativity it enabled, which ultimately inspired me to study CS

From 2021-2025, I completed my undergraduate degree at San Diego State University. At SDSU, I studied a wide range of topics, including Algorithms & Data Structures, Computer Networks, Computer Security, Cloud Computing, UNIX System Administation, Database Theory and Implementation, Machine Learning, and Data Science, among others. During my undergraduate program, I placed a strong emphasis on my academic performance. While consistently working 20 hours a week and leading a student organization at SDSU, I maintained a 3.93 GPA in major-related courses. In May of 2025, I obtained my Bachelor's of Science, graduating Summa Cum Laude. I was also identified as one of the 2025 Outstanding Computer Science Undergraduate Student by the department.

This fall, I'll begin pursuing my Master's of Science at UC Davis. One of the things that I love about computer science its breadth. Computer Science is a diverse field spanning topics, and if I could, I'd study them all. However, while pursuing my master's, my topics of focus will be Software Engineering, Programming Languages, and Computer Science Education.

Skills

I'm most proficient in Python, Java, and C/C++, and am experienced with version control using Git and Github. I have a good working knowledge of HTML, CSS, JavaScript, MySQL, and Bash. I'm also familiar with containerization tools like Docker, and am generally quite comfortable in a UNIX environment. Moreover, I've worked with Google Cloud Platform tools such as Google Cloud Run, IAM, and Cloud SQL.

Work Experience

SAASS Tutoring

At the start of my second year at SDSU, I began working part-time as a tutor for SDSU's Student-Athlete Academic Support Services (SAASS) team. Here, I gained valuable practice teaching fundamental computer science concepts, and identified a passion for teaching computer science. As a part of this team, I scheduled and led one-on-one tutoring sessions, prepared lesson materials in advance, and created relevant visualizations. This role also helped me to develop my own understanding of fundamental computer science topics, and inspired me to further study computer science education.

The IT User Services Help Desk

Also in Fall 2022, I began working as a student analyst for SDSU's IT Division, and was later promoted to a lead student analyst in the summer of 2024 on account of performance. As a part of IT User Services, my team provided frontline support for the more than 75,000+ active students, staff, and faculty. Our team provided support for technical issues related to Google Workspace, Microsoft Office 365, Duo Mobile, Adobe Sign/Creative Cloud and more through the ServiceNow Platform. During my time in this role, I resolved 4,000+ support requests comprising more than 15% of all resquests routed to the IT division. Beyond just technical support however, this role provided me with valuable insight into broader IT responsibilities including infrastructure management, SaaS platform administration and integration, and cross-directory account synchronization.

Research at the AI4Business Lab

In the fall of 2023, I joined a research team at SDSU's AI4Business Lab, where we examined the existing landscape of Large Language Models and further investigated their capabilities in business-related contexts. As part of my responsibilies, I collaborated on the development of several Python scripts aimed at solving NLP-related tasks. These tasks included tokenization, text sanitization, n-gram frequency analysis. One of the things that I quite enjoyed about working on these scripts was that it also gave me some additional practice with remote development, as the high computational power required for AI training necessitated the use of a private cloud environment for development and exection.
